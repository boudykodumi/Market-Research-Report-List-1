<p><p>The PbTe-based thermoelectric material market is primarily divided into two types: N-type and P-type. N-type materials are characterized by the presence of excess electrons, enhancing their electrical conductivity, making them suitable for high-temperature applications. In contrast, P-type materials have holes as charge carriers, leading to different electronic properties and optimal performance under varying thermal conditions. Together, these materials are essential for efficient thermoelectric devices, enabling energy conversion in applications like waste heat recovery and refrigeration.</p></p>
