<p><ul><li>External Fixation Systems</li><li>Internal Fixation Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Small Bone Fixation Systems market encompasses two primary types: external and internal fixation systems. External fixation systems involve the use of rods and pins attached outside the body to stabilize fractures, often utilized for severe injuries or infections. Internal fixation systems, on the other hand, involve devices such as plates, screws, and nails inserted within the body to directly stabilize fractured bones. Both systems aim to promote healing while minimizing complications, with specific applications depending on the injury type and location.</p></p>
