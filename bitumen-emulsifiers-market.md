<p><ul><li>Anionic</li><li>Cationic</li><li>Non-ionic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bitumen emulsifiers market is categorized into three main types: anionic, cationic, and non-ionic emulsifiers. Anionic emulsifiers are negatively charged and are typically used in applications where the aggregates carry a positive charge. Cationic emulsifiers are positively charged, suitable for negatively charged aggregates, providing enhanced stability. Non-ionic emulsifiers do not carry a charge, offering versatility and compatibility in various formulations. Each type plays a crucial role in achieving desired properties and performance in bitumen emulsions for construction and road maintenance applications.</p></p>
